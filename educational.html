Educational Health Seminar Reflection Page

Participating in the Educational Health Seminar was a profoundly meaningful experience. Our group conducted an educational session among young orphans from Batinguel, Dumaguete City, focusing on promoting health awareness through interactive and age-appropriate activities.

Unlike classroom-based demonstrations, this outreach involved children of varied ages and learning capacities, ranging from early childhood to adolescence. To ensure active participation, we divided the participants into smaller groups and facilitated hands-on learning activities. Our goal was to create a fun, inclusive, and educational environment where the children could learn while enjoying themselves.

This experience taught me valuable lessons about adaptability, empathy, and communication. I realized that teaching extends beyond structured lessons—it involves connecting with learners on a human level, understanding their needs, and making education accessible to all.

On a personal note, this seminar fostered my growth as a communicator and collaborator. It strengthened my confidence and reminded me of the importance of service learning—applying knowledge for the benefit of others. This activity deepened my understanding that education is not confined to classrooms; it thrives in communities, where learning becomes a shared and transformative experience.
